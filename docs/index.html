Identity

Brand foundations

Our brand blends human warmth with reliable utility. We favor clear layouts, generous white space, and thoughtful motion. Caveat adds a human note in headlines; Inter ensures functional clarity everywhere else.

  • Human, not cutesy.
  • Optimistic, not naive.
  • Helpful, never pushy.

Clarity first

Prefer obvious choices, strong hierarchy, and consistent affordances. Remove anything that doesn’t help someone decide or act.

Warm minimalism

Use generous spacing, soft contrast, and the brand color as a highlight – not paint. Motion should guide, not decorate.

Trust by default

Accessible by design. Communicate state clearly. Always offer undo or a safe path back.

Typography

Voice through type

Headlines and subheads use Caveat for a distinctive, human touch. Body, UI, and data use Inter for readability and versatility.

A friendly headline in Caveat

Use Caveat for H1–H3 and expressive callouts. Keep line length short.

Inter for workhorse UI

Inter supports dense layouts, data tables, and long-form reading. Use 1.5 line-height for paragraphs.

Type scale

  • Display 48/56 Caveat
  • H1 36/44 Caveat
  • H2 28/36 Caveat
  • H3 22/28 Inter
  • Body 16/24 Inter
  • Small 14/20 Inter

Color

Light and dark palettes

We design light-first with a soft paper tone; dark mode is deep charcoal. The brand color is warm coral. Use color to signal state and highlight key actions.

Iconography

Simple, friendly forms

Icons are rounded, 2px strokes, and solid fills for small sizes. Maintain consistent metaphor and simplicity.

Motion

Purposeful, soft motion

Use 150–250ms durations with ease-out curves for entrances and ease-in for exits. Prefer micro-interactions and subtle parallax.

Contrast

Body text meets WCAG AA in both themes. Test combinations with interactive states.

Structure

Use semantic HTML. Preserve focus order and visible focus states. Provide skip links.

Motion sensitivity

Respect reduce-motion preferences and provide non-animated alternatives.

Voice & Tone

Warm, direct, helpful

Write like a helpful teammate. Prefer verbs to nouns. Choose everyday words. Be concise and kind.

  • Do “Save changes”
  • Do “We’ll email you a receipt”
  • Don’t “Persist configuration”

Microcopy checklist

  • State the action first.
  • Prefer active voice.
  • Show empathy. Offer recovery.
